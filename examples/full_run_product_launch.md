# Full Run Example: Product Launch

## Scenario

**Company (fictional):** InsightLoop

InsightLoop is a small B2B SaaS company (11 people) launching a new AI-assisted customer research tool for product and growth teams in mid-market SaaS companies.

The product helps teams turn interview transcripts, support tickets, and sales call notes into structured themes, objections, and opportunity signals.

The market is crowded. Large competitors already offer broad "AI insights" features inside bigger product suites. InsightLoop has:
- limited launch budget
- a small team
- early customer interest from pilot users
- unclear positioning versus bigger players

Leadership must decide how to launch over the next 90 days.

## Stage 0: Scope Check

- **Scope result:** In scope
- **Why:** This is a business/product/go-to-market strategy decision under uncertainty.
- **Rewritten decision statement:** "How should InsightLoop launch its AI-assisted customer research tool in the next 90 days to win early traction without overextending team and budget?"
- **Decision owner:** CEO (final call), with PMM + Product Lead as primary operators.
- **Primary objective:** Secure first 20 paying teams with sustainable activation and retention signals.
- **Obvious constraints:**
  - Launch budget cap: $45k for 90 days
  - Small GTM team (1 PMM, 1 founder, part-time designer)
  - Product still maturing on data quality for non-English transcripts
  - No enterprise sales team
- **What cannot be answered yet:**
  - True willingness to pay by segment
  - How aggressively incumbents will respond
  - Which message angle drives fastest activation

- Team has strong domain expertise in research ops but low brand awareness.

- Pressure: runway is 13 months; leadership wants paid traction this quarter.
- Distribution today: founder network, small newsletter, LinkedIn following.

### Intake summary

InsightLoop has credible early product signal but limited distribution and limited launch resources. It must choose between speed, positioning clarity, and controlled risk.

Which launch strategy should InsightLoop use in the next 90 days to achieve early paid traction with acceptable execution risk?

## Stage 4: Deep Dive

Selected options:
1. Research-Ops Niche Wedge
2. Design Partner Private Beta-to-Paid

### Option A: Research-Ops Niche Wedge

#### Strategic logic
A sharp segment-specific message increases relevance, which improves qualification quality and shortens decision cycles for buyers with urgent research synthesis pain. Higher fit should produce better activation and retention than broad positioning.

#### What must be true
- **User-side:** team can execute high-quality onboarding and messaging consistently.
- **Customer/market:** target segment has urgent recurring research workflows.
- **Competitor/partner/channel:** incumbents do not immediately neutralize the niche message.
- **Internal execution:** PMM + founder capacity can sustain demand and implementation.

#### Execution path
1. Finalize ICP definition and disqualifiers.
2. Publish niche value narrative + proof-backed landing page.
3. Run targeted outbound to 120 ICP accounts.
4. Launch weekly founder-led demo sessions.
5. Onboard first 15 paid teams with structured activation milestones.
6. Publish first 2 case studies with quantified outcomes.
- **Time to first signal:** 2-3 weeks.
- **Time to meaningful result:** 8-10 weeks.

#### Required resources

| Resource | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Budget for campaign + content | available | within $45k cap |
| PMM execution bandwidth | partially available | single PMM is bottleneck |
| Founder demo capacity | available | but limited for scale |
| Data instrumentation | partially available | event tracking needs cleanup |
| Customer onboarding support | partially available | CSM support thin |
| Brand trust in niche | unknown | early pilots positive but small sample |

#### Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Severity | Early warning signal | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Segment too narrow for target pace | Medium | High | pipeline volume below weekly target | expand adjacent sub-segment after 4 weeks |
| Message not sufficiently differentiated | Medium | High | low demo request rate despite outreach | sharpen proof points and claims |
| Onboarding bottleneck harms activation | High | Medium | activation delays >14 days | add onboarding templates + office hours |

#### Weak points
- Heavy dependence on consistent execution by a very small GTM team.
- Narrow focus may constrain short-term volume.
- Differentiation relies on quality proof that is still being built.

#### First tests / experiments

1. **ICP Resonance Test**
   - Assumption: niche message materially improves reply/demo rates.
   - Run: A/B outbound messaging across 120 accounts.
   - Pass: niche message yields >40% lift in qualified demo rate.
   - Fail: no meaningful lift after 3 weeks.
   - Effort: Medium.

2. **Activation Reliability Sprint**
   - Assumption: onboarding can deliver value in <10 days.
   - Run: instrument 15-team onboarding cohort.
   - Pass: >=70% reach first insight milestone in 10 days.
   - Fail: <50% milestone completion.
   - Effort: Medium-High.

#### Invalidators
- Qualified pipeline remains below threshold for 4 consecutive weeks.
- Activation milestone failure persists after onboarding changes.
- Competitor offers equivalent niche workflow bundled at effectively zero incremental price.

#### Updated confidence
Medium
- Increased by: strong fit with constraints and clearer differentiation path.
- Reduced by: limited bandwidth + uncertain segment ceiling.
- Evidence most needed: repeatable qualified acquisition and activation consistency.

### Option B: Design Partner Private Beta-to-Paid

#### Strategic logic
A controlled private cohort enables product and onboarding hardening, creating credible references and reducing public launch failure risk. Strong early outcomes can become launch assets.

#### What must be true
- **User-side:** team can keep tight feedback loops and deliver hands-on support.
- **Customer/market:** design partners are willing to co-build and then pay.
- **Competitor/partner/channel:** competitors do not force immediate broad launch response.
- **Internal execution:** product team can ship key fixes quickly.

#### Execution path
1. Select 12 design partners matching strict ICP.
2. Set explicit success criteria and shared implementation plan.
3. Run 6-week guided beta with weekly check-ins.
4. Convert successful partners to paid annual/monthly plans.
5. Build 3 publishable case studies.
6. Launch broader campaign using validated outcomes.
- **Time to first signal:** 2 weeks.
- **Time to meaningful result:** 8-12 weeks.

#### Required resources

| Resource | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Budget | available | lower cash burn than broad campaign |
| Onboarding + CS capacity | partially available | manageable at 12-partner cap |
| Product iteration velocity | available | current sprint capacity supports fixes |
| Case study production capability | partially available | PMM + founder time needed |
| Partner quality pipeline | unknown | not all pilot accounts meet criteria |

#### Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Severity | Early warning signal | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Too slow for revenue expectations | Medium | High | delayed paid conversion timeline | define conversion checkpoints at week 4/6 |
| Partners consume excessive support | Medium | Medium | support hours exceed plan by >30% | enforce scoped onboarding playbook |
| No strong case-study outcomes | Medium | High | weak measurable impact by week 6 | narrow use cases and tighten success criteria |

#### Weak points
- Risk of perceived "soft launch" internally if revenue pressure increases.
- Success depends on selecting the right partners, not just willing ones.
- Can delay broader market learning if overextended.

#### First tests / experiments

1. **Design Partner Selection Test**
   - Assumption: strict partner criteria predict better conversion outcomes.
   - Run: score pilot pool and onboard top 12 only.
   - Pass: >=60% of cohort reaches value milestone in 14 days.
   - Fail: broad underperformance regardless of criteria.
   - Effort: Medium.

2. **Paid Conversion Trigger Test**
   - Assumption: measurable outcome narrative drives paid conversion.
   - Run: conversion conversation at week 6 with quantified ROI artifact.
   - Pass: >=35% partner-to-paid conversion first cycle.
   - Fail: <20% conversion.
   - Effort: Medium.

#### Invalidators
- Conversion below threshold after two iterations.
- Support burden blocks core roadmap execution.
- Revenue gap becomes incompatible with runway targets.

#### Updated confidence
Medium
- Increased by: strong quality and proof-building logic.
- Reduced by: timing risk against near-term commercial pressure.
- Evidence most needed: conversion velocity from partner to paid.

## Stage 5: Decision Summary

### Executive summary

InsightLoop must choose a launch path that balances commercial urgency with execution realism. The chosen working strategy is a staged hybrid: design-partner private beta-to-paid first, followed by a focused niche wedge launch. This path best fits limited resources while improving odds of durable retention and stronger proof before wider exposure. It was preferred over more aggressive or broader plays due to lower reputational and execution risk. The largest remaining uncertainty is whether conversion velocity will be fast enough to satisfy 90-day traction goals.

### Chosen working strategy

- **Strategy:** Staged Hybrid (Design Partner -> Niche Wedge Launch)
- **Explanation:** Convert a tightly selected partner cohort into paid references, then launch publicly to a narrow high-fit segment with proof-backed positioning.
- **Intended outcome:** 20 paying teams with at least 60% 30-day active usage.
- **Time horizon:** 90-day decision window; first proof in 4-6 weeks.
- **Confidence:** Medium
- **Uncertainty note:** Commercial pace may lag if partner conversion or outbound resonance is weaker than expected.

### Why this strategy was chosen

- Strong fit with current team capacity and budget constraints.
- Balances quality/retention with need for near-term traction.
- Creates defensible differentiation through concrete outcomes rather than generic AI claims.
- Reduces risk of public overpromising against larger incumbents.
- Preserves optionality to accelerate launch if early proof is strong.

### Rejected or deferred options

| Option | Status | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Partner/Channel-Led Launch | Deferred | Cycle length too slow for current quarter goals |
| Direct Competitor Comparison Campaign | Rejected (for now) | High downside and reputation risk with limited proof base |
| Founder-Led Thought Leadership (as primary) | Deferred | Good support channel, weak as stand-alone conversion engine |
| Pure Niche Wedge immediate full launch | Deferred | Chosen as phase 2 after stronger proof is built |

### Trade-offs accepted

- Accept slower top-line growth now for stronger retention and proof.
- Accept higher short-term team attention cost in onboarding.
- Prioritize trust and defensibility over aggressive awareness spikes.
- Trade some speed for reduced launch failure risk.

### Assumptions behind the decision

| Assumption | Why it matters | Confidence | Test/monitor |
|---|---|---|---|
| Design partners will convert at >=35% | Needed to hit revenue pace | Medium | week-6 conversion checkpoints |

- True willingness-to-pay ceiling by sub-segment.
